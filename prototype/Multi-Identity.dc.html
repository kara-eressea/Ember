Connecting with multiple identities

Each connected identity is its own live session — separate presence, DMs, pinned channels and roles — so switching between them is a top-level context switch, not a filter. Here are the two ways to expose that switch in the client. Both are shown on your 2b Dusk Purple base with three identities connected: Dorn Vael (active), nightjar (3 unread), ember_ops (a mention).

3aIdentity railfar-left columnrecommended
A slim vertical rail on the far left — active identity squares off, others stay circular with presence dots and unread/mention badges. Scales past 2–3 identities, keeps the channel header clean, and reads as a persistent “who am I right now.” Familiar from Discord’s guild rail, repurposed for characters.
3bIdentity tabstop bar
A tab strip above the channel header — active tab is lifted with an accent underline and a close (disconnect) affordance. Very legible for 2–3 identities since names are always visible, but it eats vertical space and crowds once you pass a handful.

Pick a direction — 3a or 3b — and I’ll fold it into the main client and wire the identity switch into the per-identity sidebar (pins, DMs, presence all swap with the active identity).
